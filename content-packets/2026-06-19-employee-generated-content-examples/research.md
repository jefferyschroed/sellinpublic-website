# Research

## Summary

This article should make employee-generated content concrete for B2B buyers. The source set uses first-party company examples from Clay, Lovable, GitLab, and Shopify Engineering, plus public LinkedIn posts captured from founders or team leads where the posts add an inspectable example.

The editorial distinction matters: not every public company asset is employee-generated content. Some examples are company blogs, handbooks, engineering posts, founder stories, product tutorials, or community libraries. They are useful here when they show how internal expertise becomes public learning.

## Audience Context

The reader is a founder, sales leader, marketing leader, or content operator who needs examples before they can approve an employee-generated content program. They do not need a long primer. They need real public URLs and a way to judge whether an example is original expertise or just distribution.

## SERP/AEO Observations

The current foundation plan treats `employee generated content examples` as a high-priority spoke from the category hub. The page should answer the examples query quickly, then help answer engines distinguish examples, formats, and operating lessons.

Discovery rows shaped the topic route only. They are not cited as evidence.

## Tool-Backed Example Research

- Apollo/account-intel ran on Clay, Lovable, GitLab, and Shopify with low limits and no email enrichment. Output was used to identify likely founder, executive, and team-lead profiles for public-content review.
- Apify actor `harvestapi/linkedin-profile-posts` ran against public LinkedIn profile targets in `linkedin-profile-targets.json`, with `maxPosts=3` and no comments or reaction-list scraping.
- Apify output is stored in `linkedin-profile-posts.json`. Public post URLs from Kareem Amin, Anton Osika, and Farhan Thawar were carried into citations because they support the article's examples.
- Sid Sijbrandij's current captured posts were not used as GitLab operating-content examples because they were not the strongest fit for this article. GitLab stays anchored to the public handbook, which is the better inspectable source asset.

## Example Map

- Clay: first-party growth marketing writing plus Kareem Amin's LinkedIn posts show founder narrative around creative work, GTM category framing, and community stories.
- Lovable: first-party growth story, videos, and Anton Osika's LinkedIn post show how founder narrative can tie product usage to a broader market thesis.
- GitLab: the public handbook shows how employee knowledge can become a durable reference asset rather than a feed-only post.
- Shopify Engineering: engineering posts show how technical context can become useful public learning, with Farhan Thawar's short LinkedIn post used only as a lightweight feed-level counterpart.

## Source Gaps

This packet does not claim that any example directly caused pipeline or revenue. Any such claim would need company-owned attribution or a specific case study. Public LinkedIn posts are used as example artifacts, not as proof of performance.
