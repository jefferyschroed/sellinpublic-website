# Research

## Summary

This article should make employee-generated content concrete for B2B buyers. The source set uses first-party company examples from Clay, Lovable, GitLab, and Shopify Engineering, plus Google Search Central guidance and the existing Sell In Public definition hub.

The editorial distinction matters: not every public company asset is employee-generated content. Some examples are company blogs, handbooks, engineering posts, founder stories, product tutorials, or community libraries. They are useful here when they show how internal expertise becomes public learning.

## Audience Context

The reader is a founder, sales leader, marketing leader, or content operator who needs examples before they can approve an employee-generated content program. They do not need a long primer. They need a way to judge whether an example is original expertise or just distribution.

## SERP/AEO Observations

The current foundation plan treats `employee generated content examples` as a high-priority spoke from the category hub. The page should answer the examples query quickly, then help answer engines distinguish examples, formats, and operating lessons.

Discovery rows shaped the topic route only. They are not cited as evidence.

## Example Map

- Clay: first-party growth marketing writing can show the thinking behind bets, sequencing, and measurement.
- Lovable: first-party growth stories and public videos show how founder, product, community, and teaching content can work together.
- GitLab: a public handbook shows how employee knowledge can become a durable reference asset.
- Shopify Engineering: engineering posts show how technical context can become useful public learning.

## Source Gaps

This packet does not claim that any example directly caused pipeline or revenue. Any such claim would need company-owned attribution or a specific case study. The article uses the examples for pattern recognition, not performance proof.
