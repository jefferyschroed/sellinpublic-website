# SME Notes

Packet: `2026-06-19-employee-generated-content-vs-employee-advocacy`

## Sell In Public POV

Employee-generated content and employee advocacy are close enough that buyers often treat them as interchangeable. The operating difference matters. Advocacy usually asks employees to help distribute a company message. Employee-generated content starts with what employees know from the work itself.

The practical rule:

- If the company already has the message and needs reach, use advocacy.
- If the company needs sharper public expertise, use employee-generated content.
- If a launch, report, or major point of view needs human context, combine both.

## Editorial Constraints

- Do not imply employee advocacy is a weak model.
- Do not claim employee-generated content is automatically more authentic or higher-performing.
- Do not tell every employee to post.
- Do not reduce LinkedIn content to posting volume.
- Keep the explanation useful for founders, sales leaders, marketing leaders, and content operators.

## Measurement POV

Advocacy can be measured with participation, reach, clicks, and campaign lift. Employee-generated content also needs usefulness signals: saves, comments from relevant people, replies, buyer questions, examples reused by sales, owned-page search visibility, and answer-engine citation opportunities.

Those signals should guide refreshes and future briefs. They should not be presented as guaranteed revenue attribution.
