## Source Synthesis

The article should make an operating-model distinction, not a winner-take-all argument.

Founder-led content is strongest when the company needs a clear market stance, category argument, or executive narrative. The founder can say why the company sees the market differently. Dave Gerhardt's public posts are useful examples because they do not read like campaign copy. They connect a business owner, a market view, and a specific buyer conversation.

Employee-generated content is strongest when the buyer needs proof from people doing the work. The ClickUp and Microsoft examples show two different versions of this: ClickUp's post emphasizes individual practitioners creating under their own names, while Microsoft's post emphasizes a structured program with strategy, tools, and creator freedom.

LinkedIn's current platform guidance also matters. Its AI visibility article recommends customer-question-led content, employee participation, clear POV, consistent terminology, and structured writing. Its Help docs show that thought leader ads can sponsor member posts with author permission, while separate Help docs show the older Employee Advocacy tab began being discontinued in November 2024. That means teams should treat employee content and employee advocacy as operating models, not as one native LinkedIn feature.

The Edelman-LinkedIn 2025 B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report supports the broader buyer-behavior context. It does not prove founder posts or employee posts directly create pipeline. It does support the claim that B2B buyers use public expertise to evaluate how a company thinks.

## Source Limits

- Public LinkedIn posts are examples, not research studies.
- The ClickUp and Microsoft posts are self-reported practitioner artifacts. Use them to show operating patterns, not to validate performance.
- Oktopost and Sprout Social are vendor/company sources. Use them for program framing and guardrails, not independent performance proof.
- The LinkedIn AI visibility article is platform research and includes Semrush analysis. Use it for current platform guidance, with attribution.
