## Approved Operating POV

- Founder-led content and employee-generated content should be chosen by job-to-be-done, not by ego or org chart.
- Use founder-led content when the company needs a sharp market stance, category narrative, company conviction, or executive credibility.
- Use employee-generated content when buyers need to inspect the work through practitioners, sellers, product leaders, customer success, engineers, or other subject-matter experts.
- Use a hybrid when the founder can set the point of view and employees can prove it through specific workflows, customer questions, implementation lessons, and role-level judgment.
- Do not make the founder the only content source once the company has enough subject-matter experts to show how the team thinks.
- Do not turn employees into repost machines. Employee content should be voluntary, supported, edited for accuracy, and tied to real expertise.
- Measure founder-led content by executive reply quality, target-account recognition, comments from relevant buyers, sales conversation use, branded search, invitations, and narrative consistency.
- Measure employee-generated content by relevant comments, saves, buyer questions, sales reuse, recruiting signals, source reuse, and whether it surfaces real expertise that marketing could not invent.
