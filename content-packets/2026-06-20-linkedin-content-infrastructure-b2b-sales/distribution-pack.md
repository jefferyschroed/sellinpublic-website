## LinkedIn Post Option 1

Most B2B sales teams do not have a LinkedIn content problem. They have an infrastructure problem.

The useful inputs are already there:

- buyer objections
- demo questions
- deal reviews
- implementation tradeoffs
- customer success notes
- founder POV
- rep-level field insight

The missing piece is the weekly loop that turns those inputs into public expertise and feeds the learning back to sales.

New post: LinkedIn Content Infrastructure For B2B Sales Teams

## LinkedIn Post Option 2

If LinkedIn content is treated as rep homework, it will stay random.

The better system:

1. Capture buyer questions.
2. Match each question to the right internal expert.
3. Review for risk without killing the voice.
4. Publish on a real cadence.
5. Track both LinkedIn signals and sales feedback.
6. Turn the best answers into owned content, outbound, and enablement.

That is LinkedIn content infrastructure.
