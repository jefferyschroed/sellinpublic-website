## Usable Insights

- A sales team's LinkedIn system should start with field inputs: repeated objections, deal reviews, demo questions, implementation concerns, customer wins, and category arguments.
- Voice selection should follow buyer credibility. The founder is not always the best author. A sales engineer, CSM, RevOps lead, or AE may have better standing for a narrow buyer question.
- Review should protect claims, confidentiality, and role fit without turning each post into brand copy.
- Comments and replies belong in the infrastructure. A post can create a useful buyer signal even when it does not create a direct inbound lead.
- Sales teams need a weekly feedback loop: which posts gave reps better language, surfaced objections, created replies, or earned saves from target accounts?
- Repurposing is part of the system. A strong post can become a cold email opener, a call follow-up, a website FAQ, a short article section, or an internal enablement note.
