# Distribution Pack

## LinkedIn Post Option 1

Most employee-generated content reports overrate impressions.

Impressions still matter. They tell you whether the post got reach.

But if the content is supposed to support revenue, the report also needs:

- Buyer-fit engagement.
- Real questions and replies.
- Sales reuse.
- CRM influence notes.
- Search visibility.
- AI-answer visibility checks.

The better question is not "how many people saw it?"

It is "did the right buyers trust it, use it, reply to it, search for it, or move closer to a sales conversation because of it?"

New article: How To Measure Employee-Generated Content Beyond Impressions

## LinkedIn Post Option 2

The most useful employee-generated content metric depends on the job.

If the job is awareness, qualified reach matters.

If the job is trust, buyer questions and saves matter.

If the job is pipeline, replies, meetings, CRM notes, tracked links, and sales reuse matter.

If the job is search visibility, Search Console and AI-answer checks matter.

Impressions belong in the report. They just should not own the report.
