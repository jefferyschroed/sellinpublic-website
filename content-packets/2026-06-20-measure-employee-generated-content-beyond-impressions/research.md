# Research

## Summary

The strongest evidence supports a measurement model with five layers:

1. Platform reach and engagement signals from LinkedIn.
2. Buyer-fit and trust signals that qualify whether the right people engaged.
3. Pipeline influence captured through links, replies, CRM notes, and sales activity.
4. Sales reuse, where posts become follow-up material, objection handling, or meeting prep.
5. Search and AI visibility from indexed pages, branded queries, citations, and AI-answer checks.

The article should not argue that impressions are useless. Impressions are a useful reach input. They are not enough to prove revenue, trust, pipeline influence, or search visibility.

## Audience Context

The reader is a B2B revenue leader, founder, RevOps leader, or marketing leader who already understands LinkedIn impressions and engagement. The practical problem is reporting: a post may have high reach but no useful buyer movement, or a low-reach post may create a sales conversation, a useful reply, or a search-visible answer.

## Source Notes

- LinkedIn member post analytics are current and directly relevant. LinkedIn says post analytics can show discovery, in-network and out-of-network reach, profile activity, video or article performance, social engagement, link engagement, post viewer demographics, and ad spend for boosted posts.
- LinkedIn Page content analytics are directly relevant for company-side reporting. LinkedIn lists highlights, metrics, likes, comments, reposts, and filtered views over time, with a note that some metrics can take up to 48 hours to reflect.
- LinkedIn's older employee advocacy measurement post is useful for the measurement categories: content engagement, employee activation, and business outcomes. It is older, so the public copy should use it as a category precedent, not as current product documentation.
- Google Analytics campaign URL documentation is current and directly relevant for tracking content-driven visits. Google recommends `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, and `utm_campaign`, with consistent names to prevent fragmented reporting.
- Google Search Console Performance report is current and directly relevant for search visibility. It reports clicks, impressions, CTR, average position, queries, pages, countries, devices, search appearance, and dates.
- Gartner's B2B buying journey page supports the idea that B2B buying is nonlinear and task-based, so content measurement should include influence and buying-task support rather than only last-click conversion.
- LinkedIn's article about Meltwater research is a first-party summary by Meltwater's Chief Product Officer on LinkedIn Marketing Solutions. It says Meltwater analyzed 9.5 million AI citations across six models, that LinkedIn ranked second behind YouTube, and that 75% of LinkedIn citations came from individual member profiles. Use this carefully as AI visibility context, not as proof that any specific employee content program will be cited.
- Meltwater's May 2026 AI search visibility report supports the broader point that AI visibility can be measured as reputation and citation visibility across trusted sources. It is not specifically about employee-generated content, so use it as adjacent visibility context.

## Entity Map

- Employee-generated content: employee, founder, seller, or subject-matter expert content based on real work and expertise.
- Employee advocacy: coordinated employee sharing or distribution of company-approved messages.
- LinkedIn analytics: post-level and Page-level reporting surfaces for reach, engagement, link activity, profile activity, and audience demographics.
- Pipeline influence: a softer attribution category that records whether content helped create, warm, revive, or support a sales conversation.
- Search visibility: organic search query, page, CTR, average position, and clicks from Search Console.
- AI visibility: inclusion or citation in AI-generated answers, measured by query checks or third-party AI visibility tooling.
