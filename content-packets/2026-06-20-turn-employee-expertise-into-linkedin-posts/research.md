# Research

Packet: `2026-06-20-turn-employee-expertise-into-linkedin-posts`

## Research Summary

The article should own a practical workflow: capture employee expertise, turn it into a specific LinkedIn post, and keep the post from sounding like generic ghostwritten personal-brand content.

The strongest sources support three separate ideas:

- LinkedIn's AI visibility guidance supports the article's structure: answer real customer questions, use clear definitions, write with readable structure, keep terminology consistent, and ground claims in expertise or evidence. This source should support the recommendation to start with one buyer question and one defensible point, not broad performance claims.
- Oktopost's employee advocacy guide is useful context for the different operating model behind advocacy: employees share company content or messages through their networks. It should be used to clarify the distinction, not to claim advocacy is bad.
- The Heike Young and Chris Cunningham LinkedIn posts are public examples. They show how named practitioners discuss employee-generated content or employee creator programs in public, but they do not prove a general benchmark.

## Audience Context

The reader is a B2B founder, CRO, VP of Sales, marketing leader, or content operator with real expertise inside the company and no consistent way to publish it. They do not need a generic explanation of LinkedIn. They need an operating workflow that answers:

- Which employees should we pull from?
- What raw material do we capture?
- How do we turn a messy expert answer into a post?
- How do we keep it accurate without making it sound sterile?
- How do we know whether the post was useful?

## Entity Map

- Employee-generated content: public content originating from employee expertise, work, or point of view.
- Employee advocacy: employee distribution of company-approved messages, assets, or campaigns.
- Subject matter expert: the employee with real knowledge, not necessarily the person doing the final edit.
- Ghostwriting: an editor or writer creating content for someone else. The problem is not writing help itself; the problem is invented generic voice and unsupported positioning.
- LinkedIn post: the public format. The workflow can also feed owned content, sales enablement, email, and answer-engine pages.
