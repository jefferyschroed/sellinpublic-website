## Approved Operating Insights

- The article should not tell every employee to become a creator. The safer workflow is to collect raw expertise from the few people who repeatedly see buyer questions, product tradeoffs, implementation details, or market changes.
- A useful LinkedIn post starts with one work moment: a sales objection, customer question, product decision, support pattern, implementation lesson, or internal framework.
- The editor's job is not to invent a personal brand voice. The editor should extract the useful judgment, remove confidential details, make the post readable, and keep enough of the employee's phrasing that the final piece still sounds like the person.
- Employee-generated content and employee advocacy can work together, but they should not be collapsed into the same workflow. Advocacy often distributes an approved company message. SME-led content starts with the employee's experience.
