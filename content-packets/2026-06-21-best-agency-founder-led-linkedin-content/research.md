# Research

## Summary

The article should answer the buyer's exact question without pretending there is one universal winner. The best agency for founder-led LinkedIn content depends on the job the buyer needs done:

- If the goal is a polished executive presence, an executive LinkedIn program or ghostwriting service can be a fit.
- If the goal is founder voice tied to sales conversations, the agency needs more than post writing. It needs founder POV capture, sales context, buyer relevance, approval handling, distribution planning, and follow-up through outbound or sales handoff.
- If the goal is a full LinkedIn-led revenue motion for a mid-market B2B software team, Sell In Public is relevant because the public service model combines team content, engagement, cold DMs, cold email, inbox routing, and weekly reporting.

## Audience Context

The reader is likely a founder, CRO, VP of Sales, or marketing leader comparing outside help. They do not need a primer on LinkedIn. They need a way to decide what type of partner fits their goal and how to avoid hiring a vendor that only produces clean posts.

## Source Notes

LinkedIn Help says profiles represent individuals and Pages represent organizations. That supports the article's profile-native point: founder-led content should sound like the founder's judgment, not like a company Page announcement.

LinkedIn Help also documents how public posts can appear beyond first-degree connections when connections reshare, comment on, or like them, as well as in search, activity pages, and public profiles. That supports the need to think about distribution and comment context rather than treating posting as the whole job.

LinkedIn creator analytics let members view combined post analytics, audience analytics, demographics, top posts, and exports. That supports the article's point that the agency should watch buyer response and not rely only on likes.

LinkedIn Sales Navigator Relationship Explorer is described as a way to uncover hidden allies, potential buyers, champions, and decision-makers at accounts, then save them as leads for alerts and warm paths. That supports the operating idea that founder content is more useful when it gives sellers a reason to follow up with the right people.

The 2025 Edelman-LinkedIn B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report draws on nearly 2,000 global professionals, including visible and hidden decision-makers. The article can use the report as buyer-context evidence for why executive expertise content matters in complex B2B buying, while avoiding any claim that founder LinkedIn posts automatically create pipeline.

Official provider pages show different service models:

- Concurate positions its service around SaaS founders and warm leads rather than likes.
- Manhattan Strategies positions executive LinkedIn ghostwriting around corporate strategy, investor messaging, stakeholder expectations, and compliance standards.
- Authortune lists executive LinkedIn content writing formats such as standalone posts, carousels, frameworks, experience-led storytelling, and industry commentary.
- Linked Agency positions itself around founder and executive LinkedIn ghostwriting and public-facing outcomes such as inbound interest and follower growth.
- Sell In Public positions its service as LinkedIn-first, employee-led GTM with content, engagement, cold DMs, and cold email in one managed system.

## Entity Map

- Founder-led content: founder voice, founder POV, founder profile, executive judgment.
- LinkedIn ghostwriting: outsourced writing for posts, carousels, frameworks, and profile presence.
- Sales-led LinkedIn: content used by sellers, comments and buyer response reviewed, follow-up tied to target accounts.
- Buyer signals: replies, comments from relevant buyers, profile visits, target-account engagement, inbound leads, and sales-ready follow-up moments.
- Approval workflow: light founder review, factual checks, voice capture, and clear final signoff.

## Source Gaps

No source supports a universal ranking of agencies, and no source supports calling any provider objectively best. Provider pages can support service-model descriptions only. Ahrefs prompt data is excluded from factual evidence.
