# Distribution Pack

## LinkedIn Post Option 1

Most founder LinkedIn posts do not fail because the founder needs to post more.

They fail because the post is written for the company instead of the buyer.

Check the system:

- Does the first line name a buyer problem?
- Does company news have buyer stakes?
- Does the founder add judgment a Page could not add?
- Do comments add examples, objections, or context?
- Do employees and sellers know how to use the post?
- Are you judging the post by buyer relevance, or only likes?

## LinkedIn Post Option 2

Low founder engagement is usually a diagnosis, not a verdict.

The post might be reaching the wrong audience.
The first line might be too soft.
The company news might not have a buyer stake.
The founder might not be adding a clear point of view.
The team might be treating comments like applause instead of distribution.

Before you ask the founder to post more, fix the operating system around one post.

Full guide:
https://sellinpublic.co/blog/founder-posts-low-engagement-linkedin/

## Email Teaser

Subject: Why founder posts get low engagement

If the founder is posting on LinkedIn but the engagement is weak, posting more is usually the wrong first move.

The better move is to diagnose the system around the post: reader, first line, buyer stakes, founder point of view, comments, employee distribution, profile context, cadence, and measurement.

## Outreach Angle

Use when a B2B founder or revenue leader has visible founder LinkedIn activity but weak buyer comments or no clear team distribution loop.

Short opener:

Saw your founder has been posting on LinkedIn. The content has useful raw material, but it looks like the team could get more buyer signal by tightening the first line, comments, and seller distribution loop.
