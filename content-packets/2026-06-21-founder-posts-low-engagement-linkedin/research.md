# Research Summary

## Search Intent

The query "Why are our founder posts getting low engagement on LinkedIn?" is diagnostic. The reader is not asking what LinkedIn is, how to create a profile, or whether founders should post. They already have founder content in motion and want to know why the work is not creating useful attention.

The answer should separate three problems:

- The post problem: weak first line, company-first topic, no founder-owned point of view, or unclear buyer stake.
- The distribution problem: no comment plan, no employee loop, no sales follow-up, or no reason for the right people to see it.
- The measurement problem: judging founder content by likes or raw impressions when the real goal is buyer relevance, profile visits, saves, sends, target-account replies, and sales reuse.

## Source Findings

LinkedIn Help says member post analytics include impressions, in-network and out-of-network impressions, members reached, profile viewers from the post, followers gained, reactions, comments, reposts, saves, sends, link visits, and viewer demographics. It also notes that analytics are estimates and may not be precise. This supports a measurement section that moves beyond likes without inventing hidden metrics. [cite:src-001]

LinkedIn combined post analytics let members evaluate portfolio performance over a date range, compare impressions and engagements, export results, and review in-network versus out-of-network exposure. This supports the argument that one weak post is less useful than a pattern review. [cite:src-002]

LinkedIn Help distinguishes profiles and Pages: profiles represent individuals, while Pages represent organizations. This supports a founder profile versus company Page section. [cite:src-003]

LinkedIn Page analytics define engagement rate as interactions divided by impressions, with interactions including clicks, reactions, comments, and shares. This supports a note that Page metrics and member profile metrics are related but not the same operating surface. [cite:src-004]

LinkedIn Help says posts set to Anyone may appear to first-degree connections, second- or third-degree connections when reshared, commented on, or liked by first-degree connections, topic search results, the activity page, and the public profile. This supports a comment and employee distribution section. [cite:src-005]

LinkedIn Help says feed systems use profile details, viewed content, and platform interactions to surface relevant posts, while reducing weak or unsafe content. This supports a careful relevance argument without pretending to know the algorithm. [cite:src-006]

LinkedIn Marketing Blog guidance for Page posts says not every Page post should be about the company or product and recommends point-of-view posts on timely or debated topics. This is adjacent to founder content, but useful for the company-news section. [cite:src-007]

The 2025 Edelman-LinkedIn report surveyed 1,934 global business executives in the United States, fielded March 17 to April 3, 2025, with a margin of error of plus or minus 2.0%. It defines expertise content as content that offers expertise, guidance, or a unique point of view, including social media posts. It says 64% of hidden decision-makers and 63% of target decision-makers spend more than one hour per week consuming this type of content. It also says 56% of target decision-makers and 55% of hidden decision-makers use expertise content as part of vendor vetting. These findings support the buyer-relevance argument, not a promise that any founder post will perform. [cite:src-008]

## Entity Map

- Founder-led content: executive judgment published from an individual profile.
- LinkedIn reach: impressions, members reached, in-network and out-of-network exposure.
- LinkedIn comments: public replies that can extend visibility and reveal buyer objections.
- LinkedIn post engagement: reactions, comments, reposts, saves, sends, clicks, and related profile activity.
- Buyer relevance: attention from the roles and accounts the company wants to reach.
- Employee-generated content: team expertise from people close to customer, product, implementation, or sales work.
- Company Page versus founder profile: organization presence versus individual professional presence.
- Sales-led LinkedIn: content used by sellers to warm target accounts, start conversations, and identify buyer signals.

## Source Gaps

No approved source was found that proves a single universal cause of low founder-post engagement. The article should present causes as diagnostic patterns and Sell In Public POV, with platform facts used only where direct.

No Ahrefs volume, rank, click, or AI-search data is published. The Ahrefs prompt shapes the search question only.
