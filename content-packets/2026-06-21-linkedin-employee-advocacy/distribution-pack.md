# Distribution Pack

## LinkedIn Post Option 1

Most employee advocacy programs on LinkedIn ask too little of the system and too much of the employee.

"Please like and reshare this company post" is not a revenue motion.

A better model starts with one buyer-relevant moment, picks the employees who have standing to participate, gives them context, and lets them choose the right action: comment, reshare, send privately, reply, or skip.

The point is not more employee activity. The point is credible participation around the moments buyers actually care about.

## LinkedIn Post Option 2

Employee advocacy and employee-generated content are not the same job.

Advocacy helps a message travel.
Employee-generated content creates original public content from what an employee knows.

When teams mix those up, they either turn employees into brand amplifiers or try to make every person a creator.

The better question is simpler: what buyer moment deserves participation this week, and who on the team has the credibility to add context?

## Email Teaser
