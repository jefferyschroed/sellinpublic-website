# Research

## Summary

LinkedIn employee advocacy is a coordinated way for employees to help a company message travel through their professional networks. The useful version for B2B revenue teams is not a mass request to reshare the same company post. It is a weekly participation loop around relevant buyer moments: which company post needs reach, which employee has standing to comment or share, what context they need, what they can personalize, and how the team will read signals afterward.

The article should keep the distinction from employee-generated content explicit. Employee advocacy is coordinated distribution and participation. Employee-generated content is employee expertise turned into original public content. This post can link to the existing EGC hub and comparison post without re-teaching the whole category.

## Source Notes

LinkedIn's employee advocacy explainer defines the category around employees promoting the company and frames a program as strategic, sustainable, and voluntary. It is older, so use it for definition and operating concepts, not current feature availability.

LinkedIn Help is current enough for feature status. It says the My Company tab, Employee Advocacy tab, and curator admin role began being discontinued in November 2024. It also says Page admins can still reshare employee posts and notify employees about important Page posts.

LinkedIn's Employee Notifications Help page explains how Page admins can notify employees and notes that employees can react, comment, or reshare after clicking the post preview. It also says admins can notify employees once per day, while LinkedIn Pages Best Practices recommends notifying employees of the most important Page posts up to one time per week. The article should treat the weekly cadence as the practical recommendation and the Help page as feature mechanics.

LinkedIn post analytics documentation supports the measurement section. It lists metrics such as impressions, members reached, profile viewers, followers gained, reactions, comments, reposts, saves, sends, link visits, and demographics, while noting that post analytics are estimates.

The 2025 Edelman-LinkedIn thought leadership research supports the revenue-team rationale. Hidden buyers and target buyers both use thought leadership during vendor evaluation, and hidden buyers may not be easy for sales to reach directly. Do not stretch this into an employee advocacy performance claim.

LinkedIn's AI-search article supports an operating recommendation: start with customer pain points and pull questions from sales conversations, customer success notes, support tickets, reviews, and win/loss notes. It also recommends choosing employees with hands-on experience when building internal advocacy.
