## Usable Insights

- Treat LinkedIn employee advocacy as a revenue participation loop, not a company-wide amplification chore.
- Participation should stay voluntary and specific. Employees need enough context to add a real point of view, comment intelligently, or share with a buyer-relevant note.
- The operating distinction matters: employee advocacy distributes and participates around a message, while employee-generated content turns employee expertise into original public content.
- Revenue teams need a buyer-signal readout after advocacy moments. Likes and impressions alone do not tell sales whether the right accounts noticed, asked questions, visited profiles, saved a post, or gave reps a useful follow-up reason.
