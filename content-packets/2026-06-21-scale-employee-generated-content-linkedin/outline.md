# Outline

## Search Promise

Answer "how to scale employee generated content on LinkedIn" with a practical operating model for a B2B revenue team.

## H1

How To Scale Employee-Generated Content On LinkedIn

## Answer-First Summary

Scale employee-generated content on LinkedIn by choosing the right employees, capturing their expertise, turning it into profile-native posts, running a light review workflow, distributing through the right people, measuring buyer response, and keeping the weekly cadence small enough to repeat.

## Structure

### Intro

Open with the operating problem: companies ask more employees to post before they build the support system.

### Short Answer

Direct answer within 150 words. Include the distinction from employee advocacy.

### Why Scaling EGC Is A System Design Problem

Use LinkedIn's current guidance on sales and customer questions plus employees with hands-on experience. Explain the difference between expert input and central production support.

### Employee-Generated Content Is Not The Same As Advocacy

Comparison table:

- Starting point
- Who speaks
- Central support
- Governance
- Measurement

Internal link to `/blog/employee-generated-content-vs-employee-advocacy/` and `/blog/linkedin-employee-advocacy/`.

### Choose The People Before You Choose The Topics

Explain the first cohort: founder, sales leader, SME, AE, CSM, product marketer. Activation rate matters more than headcount.

### Capture Expertise In A Weekly Input Loop

Inputs: sales calls, customer success notes, support tickets, win/loss notes, internal SME interviews, field objections.

Internal link to `/blog/turn-employee-expertise-into-linkedin-posts/`.

### Turn Expertise Into Profile-Native Posts

Explain post shapes: field note, objection answer, buyer mistake, customer lesson, product tradeoff, internal how-we-think note. Preserve voice with review from the person posting.

### Use A Light Review Workflow

Review for accuracy, customer sensitivity, product claims, disclosure, and platform rules. Use FTC and LinkedIn policies for support.

### Distribute Through The Right Team Members

Use personal profiles, relevant comments, private sends, and occasional Page notifications. Avoid engagement pods and forced reshares.

### Measure Buyer Response

Use useful signals: target-account engagement, comments, profile views, DMs, inbound leads, meetings influenced, rep follow-up reasons, and sales reuse.

Internal link to `/blog/measure-employee-generated-content-beyond-impressions/`.

### Weekly Workflow

Give roles, inputs, outputs, and measurement:

- Revenue owner
- Editorial operator
- SME/poster
- Reviewer
- Sales follow-up owner
