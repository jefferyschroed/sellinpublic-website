# Research

## Summary

The article should answer the query directly: scale employee-generated content on LinkedIn by building a small operating system around people, expertise capture, profile-native writing, review, distribution, and measurement. The useful angle is that scale does not mean asking every employee to post. It means creating enough central support that busy experts can contribute without becoming content operators.

## Audience Context

The reader is a founder, CRO, VP of Sales, or revenue operator at a mid-market B2B company. They may already have founder posts, a company Page, a marketing calendar, or an employee advocacy tool, but they lack a way to turn team expertise into steady LinkedIn profile content.

## Source Notes

- LinkedIn Help says the My Company tab, Employee Advocacy tab, and curator admin role began being discontinued in November 2024. Page admins can still reshare employee mentions and posts, and notify employees about important Page posts. This supports the recommendation to build a normal LinkedIn workflow rather than depending on one retired advocacy surface. [src-linkedin-advocacy-retired]
- LinkedIn Help says Employee Notifications can alert associated employees to important Page posts, with limits and employee opt-out. This supports using notifications sparingly and treating them as one distribution aid, not the whole program. [src-linkedin-employee-notifications]
- LinkedIn Marketing guidance from June 9, 2026 recommends pulling questions from sales and customer success conversations, support tickets, and win/loss notes. It also recommends starting with employees who have hands-on experience. This supports the capture and participant-selection model. [src-linkedin-ai-search]
- Edelman and LinkedIn's 2025 hidden-buyer article says target buyers and hidden buyers consume and evaluate thought leadership in similar ways, including vendor evaluation. This supports measuring buyer response beyond visible engagement. [src-edelman-linkedin-hidden-buyers]
- FTC guidance says employees who mention an employer's products on social media should disclose the relationship, and a profile listing is not enough. This supports a simple disclosure and approval lane for posts that endorse the company or its products. [src-ftc-endorsement]
- LinkedIn Professional Community Policies require real, authentic information, prohibit false or misleading content, and warn against coordinated artificial engagement. This supports a governance loop that reviews claims and avoids engagement pods. [src-linkedin-community-policies]

## Entity Map

- Employee-generated content: original content shaped from employee expertise, field experience, buyer conversations, and work-specific points of view.
- Employee advocacy: coordinated participation around company, campaign, team, or market messages.
- Founder-led content: a related voice lane that often supplies category belief and company POV.
- Team expertise: the raw material captured through SME interviews, sales notes, customer-success notes, support tickets, and win/loss notes.
- Content governance: the light review layer for factual accuracy, customer sensitivity, product claims, legal risk, and disclosure.
- Buyer signals: visible and private responses that show the content reached the right audience.

## Search And AEO Observations

The assigned AEO prompt is phrased as a practical how-to. It should not open with a definition-only answer. The first answer block should give the steps and then define the employee-generated content versus employee advocacy distinction.
