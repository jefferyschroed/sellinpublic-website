## Usable Insights

- Scaling employee-generated content is a systems problem before it is a writing problem.
- The useful weekly unit is small: a handful of credible employee voices, a short expertise capture lane, a draft queue, a light approval loop, and a sales follow-up review.
- Central support should do the operational work: topic selection, interview capture, drafting, light editing, scheduling support, repurposing, and measurement.
- Employees should keep control of voice, examples, claims, and comfort level. Their profile should not become a brand channel.
- The best early participants are people with repeated buyer exposure or category expertise: founder, sales leader, customer success lead, product marketer, sales engineer, top AE, or SDR manager.
- Measurement should look at the right audience and sales usefulness before broad reach. Useful signals include target-account engagement, comments with buyer language, profile views, DMs, sales mentions, inbound leads, and posts that give reps a strong follow-up reason.
