# Research Notes

## Summary

This post studies three public content loops rather than three isolated examples.

Clay shows a workflow-to-content loop. Clay University publishes Claybooks with named use cases, authors, templates, tools used, and step-by-step workflow context. Clay's own blog also turns internal growth work into public systems writing, including the article on how its growth team uses Clay for SEO and AEO.

Lovable shows a builder-to-public-surface loop. Lovable Launched gave builders a place to publish apps for discovery and voting, while Lovable docs explain remixing, project access, and the split between published apps and editor/code access. Lovable's April 2026 incident post is also a useful public artifact because the team explained what happened, what changed, and how public project defaults were being corrected.

GitLab shows an operating-knowledge-to-handbook loop. Its public handbook and TeamOps pages explain "public by default," while handbook development docs and communication guidance show that the handbook is backed by repos, merge requests, and a documented path for moving knowledge out of private docs.

## Audience Context

The reader is a B2B revenue leader, founder, or marketing leader who has seen public content examples but needs to understand the system behind them. The practical lesson is that public authority usually needs three parts:

1. A source of real work.
2. A public artifact buyers can inspect.
3. A route for feedback, reuse, correction, or contribution.

## Source Notes

### Clay

- Clay University's Claybook for tracking job changes shows a public workflow artifact with a named community creator, tools used, a template link, and step-by-step workflow details.
- Clay's "How Clay Uses Clay for SEO and AEO" article explains internal growth systems in public. It is useful as a public systems artifact, not as proof that the article caused business outcomes.
- Source limitation: no Clay LinkedIn post examples were cited in this packet because direct first-party pages were enough and easier to verify consistently.

### Lovable

- Lovable Launched shows a launch surface where builders could submit apps and receive votes or discovery.
- Lovable project access docs explain the public remix model and later changes to public project defaults.
- Lovable's April 2026 incident post explains a public correction around project access and public projects.
- Source limitation: Lovable community or social amplification claims are limited to what Lovable published on its own site. No social metrics are used as factual support.

### GitLab

- TeamOps shared reality explains public-by-default knowledge sharing.
- Handbook development docs show the handbook is backed by a public repository and editing workflow.
- GitLab communication guidance states that handbook pages are part of the handbook and describes the handbook as the central repository for how the company runs.
- Source limitation: GitLab pages are broad operating references. The article uses them to analyze an operating-knowledge loop, not to claim performance outcomes.
