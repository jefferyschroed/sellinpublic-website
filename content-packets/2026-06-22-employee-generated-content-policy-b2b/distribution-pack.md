# Distribution Pack

## LinkedIn Post Option 1

Most B2B teams ask for employee-generated content before they write the policy employees need.

That creates predictable problems:

- Nobody knows when to disclose the employment relationship.
- Customer names get handled by memory instead of policy.
- Product claims move faster than proof.
- Every review goes through the same slow queue.
- Employee voice gets flattened into brand copy.

The fix is not a thicker handbook. It is a practical policy with scope, disclosure, confidential-information rules, customer-reference rules, claims support, review lanes, employee-rights language, and platform conduct rules.

I wrote the checklist here: https://sellinpublic.co/blog/employee-generated-content-policy-b2b/

## LinkedIn Post Option 2

An employee-generated content policy should answer a simple question:

What can an employee publish from their own expertise, and what needs review first?

For B2B teams, the answer needs more than "use good judgment." The policy should cover disclosure, customer names, confidential information, product and AI claims, public-company topics, employee rights, workplace conduct, and LinkedIn rules.

The article includes a copyable policy checklist for revenue, marketing, HR, and legal teams.
