# Outline

## Search Promise

Give B2B revenue, marketing, HR, and legal leaders a practical employee-generated content policy model that covers disclosure, confidential information, customer names, claims, review lanes, employee rights, and platform rules without turning employees into brand channels.

## Answer-First Summary

A B2B employee-generated content policy should define who can post, what counts as personal versus company speech, when employees must disclose employment or incentives, which confidential details and customer names are off-limits, how claims get sourced, which posts need review, how platform rules apply, and where employees go for help. It should also preserve employee voice and employee rights. This is not legal advice; counsel and HR should adapt it.

## H2 Structure

1. `A Policy Should Protect Judgment, Not Flatten It`
   - Purpose and point of view.
   - Internal links to infrastructure and scaling posts.
   - Claims: C001.
2. `Start With Scope, Accounts, And Authority`
   - Personal accounts, company accounts, official spokespeople, employee profiles.
   - Company marks, handles, account ownership.
   - Sources: Stanford and Intel examples.
   - Claims: C002, C003.
3. `Make Disclosure Rules Easy To Follow`
   - Employment, incentives, sponsorship, personal benefit.
   - FTC and LinkedIn rules.
   - Claims: C004, C005, C006.
4. `Separate Green, Yellow, And Red Information`
   - Allowed public facts, review-required facts, no-post details.
   - Customer names and confidential info.
   - Sources: Intel, Stanford, Coca-Cola HBC.
   - Claims: C007, C008.
5. `Claims Need A Source Of Truth`
   - Product claims, AI claims, security claims, public-company metrics, competitive claims.
   - LinkedIn ad policy, Intel claim substantiation, SEC public-company guidance.
   - Claims: C009, C010, C011.
6. `Use Review Lanes Instead Of One Approval Queue`
   - No-review, quick review, specialist review, blocked until approved.
   - Keep speed and preserve voice.
   - Claims: C012.
7. `Protect Employee Rights And Workplace Conduct`
   - NLRB protected concerted activity.
   - EEOC harassment and workplace conduct.
   - Avoid blanket bans.
   - Claims: C013, C014.
8. `Write Platform Rules Into The Workflow`
   - LinkedIn true identity, misleading content, harassment, spam, disclosure.
   - Claims: C015.
