## LinkedIn Post Option 1

Every employee does not need to publish original LinkedIn posts.

For B2B teams, the better model is role-based:

- Credible voices publish.
- Adjacent teammates comment or send posts privately.
- Subject-matter experts feed raw insight into posts.
- Employees with poor topic fit or comfort sit out.

That is the difference between employee-generated content and a company-wide posting quota.

## LinkedIn Post Option 2

A LinkedIn program gets weaker when it asks everyone to do the same thing.

Founders, sales leaders, customer-facing operators, and subject-matter experts often have buyer context worth publishing. Other employees can still help by adding a comment, resharing with context, sending a post privately, or answering a question.

The useful question is not "How do we get everyone posting?"

It is "Who has earned the right to speak on this topic?"

## Email Teaser

Subject: Should every employee post on LinkedIn?

Most B2B teams do not need a company-wide LinkedIn posting mandate. They need a role-based participation model that protects employee voice and gives sales useful buyer signals.

## Outreach Angle

Use this post when a prospect worries that employee-generated content means asking every employee to post publicly every week.
