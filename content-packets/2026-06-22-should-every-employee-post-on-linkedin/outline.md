## Search Promise

Answer whether every employee should post on LinkedIn and give B2B leaders a practical participation model.

## Answer-First Summary

No, every employee should not be expected to publish original LinkedIn posts. A B2B company should assign LinkedIn participation by role, credibility, buyer proximity, and comfort level. Some people should publish original posts. Some should comment, reshare with context, send privately, or answer questions. Some should sit out.

4. Use a role-based participation model
   - Regular publishers.
   - Occasional participants.
   - Silent contributors.

5. Employee-generated content and employee advocacy are different decisions
   - EGC starts from expertise.
   - Advocacy coordinates participation around a message.
   - Link to internal comparison and advocacy page.
   - Claims: C005, C006.

6. Who should publish original LinkedIn posts
   - Founders, executives, sales leaders, operators, SMEs, customer-facing practitioners.
   - The test: does this person have firsthand context buyers would trust?

7. Who should participate without posting
   - Comment, reshare with context, send privately, answer comments, provide raw expertise.
   - Employee Notifications context from LinkedIn.
   - Claims: C002.

8. How to decide who sits out
   - Role risk, comfort, legal/customer sensitivity, relevance, approval burden.
   - Make sitting out a legitimate option.

9. Measure buyer signals, not employee compliance
   - LinkedIn analytics and sales context.
   - Claims: C007, C008.
