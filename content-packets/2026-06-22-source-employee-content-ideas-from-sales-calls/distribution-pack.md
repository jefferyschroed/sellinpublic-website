## LinkedIn Post Option 1

Your best employee content ideas are probably hiding in sales calls.

Not the customer names.
Not the deal details.
Not the private objections.

The useful part is one level abstracted:

- the buyer question that keeps coming back
- the objection that signals confusion
- the proof request reps keep hearing
- the phrase customers use to describe the problem
- the explanation that finally made the tradeoff clear

Capture that pattern, remove the sensitive context, and route it to the employee with standing to explain it.

## LinkedIn Post Option 2

A sales call can become a useful employee LinkedIn post only after the private details come out.

Keep the question.
Keep the objection.
Keep the explanation.
Keep the employee's judgment.

Remove the customer name, deal context, pricing, contract terms, roadmap details, security issues, and anything personal.

The goal is a post a buyer can learn from without recognizing the account behind it.

## Email Teaser

Subject: Sales calls are a content source if you abstract them first

Most teams already have useful employee content ideas in their sales calls. The hard part is turning buyer questions, objections, and proof requests into posts without exposing customer details.

This post lays out the workflow: capture the pattern, remove identifiers, check consent and retention boundaries, route the idea to the right employee, and review before posting.
