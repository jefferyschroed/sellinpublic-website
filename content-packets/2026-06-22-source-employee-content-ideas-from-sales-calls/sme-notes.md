## Approved Editorial POV

- Sales calls should be treated as content inputs, not public source material.
- The safest post idea is usually the pattern behind a call: a repeated question, objection, misunderstanding, proof request, or explanation that would help a buyer.
- Customer names, deal details, pricing, contract terms, private implementation issues, security concerns, roadmap details, and personal data should stay out of public employee posts unless the company has explicit approval and the proper review path.
- Useful employee posts should preserve the employee's judgment, not quote customers or publish call snippets.
- When a post comes from a call, route it to the person with standing to say it: AE, SDR, founder, customer success leader, solutions engineer, product marketer, or implementation lead.

## Sensitive Or Unapproved Material

- No customer quotes are approved.
- No named customer stories are approved.
- No call transcript excerpts are approved.
- No legal conclusion about recording consent is approved.

## Unresolved Questions

- Company-specific legal, compliance, and customer-contract rules vary. The public article should tell readers to follow their own policy and counsel rather than treating the post as legal advice.
