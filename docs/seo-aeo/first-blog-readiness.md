# First Blog Readiness

The first SEO/AEO blog should define the category, name the buyer problem, and connect the problem to pipeline and buyer trust. Choose a topic that can become the reference page for future posts, not a narrow tactical article.

## Recommended First Blog

Working title:

What Is Employee-Generated Content Infrastructure for B2B GTM Teams?

Why this should be first:

- Defines the category Sell In Public wants to own.
- Creates language for future internal links.
- Aligns with the homepage positioning.
- Explains the managed GTM desk without sounding like a generic LinkedIn service.
- Gives sales a page to send when prospects ask what Sell In Public actually does.

## Decision Criteria

Choose the first blog only if it:

- Defines a category Sell In Public wants to own.
- Targets a real buyer question with durable search and answer-engine value.
- Connects employee-generated content to pipeline, not vanity metrics.
- Explains the operational system: ICP, employee voices, content, engagement, outbound, reply handling, and reporting.
- Gives future blogs something to link back to.
- Supports sales conversations with prospects who ask, "What exactly do you do?"
- Can be backed by SME insight, customer patterns, sales-call language, and real operating examples.
- Avoids claims that require unavailable proof.

## Required Research Packet

Before drafting, collect:

- Target ICP and buyer roles.
- Primary search query and 5-10 related questions.
- SERP/AEO review: ranking pages, People Also Ask questions, AI answer patterns, and content gaps.
- Competitive alternatives: employee advocacy, social selling, ghostwriting, content agencies, outbound agencies, LinkedIn automation, and sales enablement.
- Sales-call language from prospects describing the problem.
- SME notes on Sell In Public's operating model.
- Examples of employee expertise becoming sales context.
- Proof points available for pipeline, meetings, replies, trust, or account engagement.
- Approved positioning language from the homepage.
- Internal links to homepage sections.
- External sources for market context, only where needed.

## SME Questions

Ask the SME:

- What buyer problem does employee-generated content infrastructure solve better than employee advocacy or ghostwriting?
- Which teams feel this pain most clearly?
- What breaks when content, outbound, and sales follow-up are managed separately?
- What does infrastructure include in practice?
- Which employee voices matter most: founders, executives, SDRs, AEs, CS, product leaders, or SMEs?
- What signals show buyer trust is increasing?
- What metrics matter before pipeline appears?
- What metrics prove the system is working?
- What objections do prospects raise?
- What examples can be shared without exposing client data?
- What claims should the blog avoid?
- What should a qualified buyer believe after reading the post?

## Launch Readiness Gates

Do not publish until all gates pass:

- Positioning gate: The article clearly defines employee-generated content infrastructure for B2B GTM teams.
- Intent gate: The article answers the core buyer question in the first section.
- Pipeline gate: Measurement language centers on replies, meetings, opportunities, account movement, and trust signals.
- Differentiation gate: The article distinguishes the category from advocacy, personal branding, generic content marketing, and outbound automation.
- Research gate: Claims are supported by SME input, buyer language, examples, or cited sources.
- AEO gate: The article includes concise answer blocks, clear definitions, question-led headings, and FAQ-ready sections.
- SEO gate: Title, meta description, H1, slug, canonical, internal links, and image alt text are ready.
- Sales gate: A GTM leader could send the post to a skeptical buyer to explain the system.
- Conversion gate: The CTA routes readers toward reviewing the system or booking a working session.
- Maintenance gate: The owner, review date, and update triggers are documented.

## Static Site Launch Checklist

Before first publication:

- Confirm `/blog/` index, post URL pattern, canonical behavior, and sitemap updates.
- Create article template with title, meta description, OG/Twitter tags, author/date, headings, internal links, CTA, and accessible images.
- Verify Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools access.
- Submit sitemap after launch and inspect the first post URL.
- QA mobile, desktop, social preview, 404 behavior, page speed, and analytics events.
- Confirm no `noindex`, blocked assets, broken internal links, or placeholder template copy.
- Add the first post to the weekly monitoring and AI citation query set.
- Schedule 7-day and 30-day post-launch reviews.
