# First Blog Readiness

The first SEO/AEO blog should define employee-generated content clearly, answer the search intent near the top, and show real examples readers can learn from.

## Recommended First Blog

Working title:

What Is Employee-Generated Content?

Why this should be first:

- Defines the category language future posts can link back to.
- Targets a durable definition query for search and answer engines.
- Shows that employee-generated content is different from employee advocacy, ghostwriting, and company-page posting.
- Uses examples from real companies instead of invented scenarios.
- Establishes the value-first editorial standard for every future post.

## Other Strong Candidates

1. Employee-Generated Content vs Employee Advocacy.
2. Examples of Employee-Generated Content From B2B Companies.
3. How To Turn Employee Expertise Into Useful LinkedIn Posts.
4. How To Measure Employee-Generated Content Beyond Impressions.

## Decision Criteria

Choose the first blog only if it:

- Defines a category Sell In Public wants to be cited for.
- Targets a real search question with durable answer-engine value.
- Explains the concept before discussing tactics.
- Includes named examples, source-backed statistics, or case studies.
- Gives future blogs something to link back to.
- Gives readers a practical checklist or framework they can use without buying anything.
- Avoids claims that require unavailable proof.
- Avoids feature-heavy service language inside explanatory sections.

## Required Research Packet

Before drafting, collect:

- Target reader and buyer roles.
- Primary search query and 5-10 related questions.
- SERP/AEO review: ranking pages, People Also Ask questions, AI answer patterns, and content gaps.
- Adjacent concepts: employee advocacy, thought leadership, social selling, creator programs, community content, and public documentation.
- Named company examples with first-party or reputable source support.
- Original research, case studies, or primary data for factual claims.
- SME notes that clarify the point of view while keeping commercial CTA language separated from the article guidance.
- Internal links to the blog index and future related articles.
- External source links for every material claim.

## SME Questions

Ask the SME:

- What is the simplest definition of employee-generated content?
- What should readers stop confusing it with?
- Which company examples are worth studying?
- What makes a piece useful instead of promotional?
- Which employee roles can contribute useful expertise?
- What should editors preserve from the employee's point of view?
- What metrics show the content is helping the right audience learn?
- What claims should the blog avoid?
- What should a reader be able to do after reading the post?

## Launch Readiness Gates

Do not publish until all gates pass:

- Definition gate: The article clearly defines employee-generated content.
- Intent gate: The article answers the core query in the first section.
- Example gate: The article includes real examples from named companies or source-backed patterns.
- Editorial-value gate: The article body is useful, source-backed, and not feature-heavy.
- Research gate: Claims are supported by SME input, examples, or cited sources.
- AEO gate: The article includes concise answer blocks, clear definitions, question-led headings, and FAQ-ready sections.
- SEO gate: Title, meta description, H1, slug, canonical, internal links, and image alt text are ready.
- CTA gate: The final CTA gives a clear next step without interrupting the article's evidence and examples.
- Maintenance gate: The owner, review date, and update triggers are documented.

## Static Site Launch Checklist

Before first publication:

- Confirm `/blog/` index, post URL pattern, canonical behavior, and sitemap updates.
- Create article template with title, meta description, OG/Twitter tags, author/date, headings, internal links, educational CTA, and accessible images.
- Verify Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools access.
- Submit sitemap after launch and inspect the first post URL.
- QA mobile, desktop, social preview, 404 behavior, page speed, and analytics events.
- Confirm no `noindex`, blocked assets, broken internal links, placeholder copy, or feature-heavy language inside explanatory sections.
- Add the first post to the weekly monitoring and AI citation query set.
- Schedule 7-day and 30-day post-launch reviews.
