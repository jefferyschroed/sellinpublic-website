Questions we hear before teams get started

What you probably want to know before booking a call.

Who actually writes the content?

We do. Our team leads strategy, interviews your people, pulls from your sales calls and customer wins, and drafts every post in the voice of whoever is posting it. You review. We publish.

Do you manage LinkedIn accounts directly?

Yes. We run the weekly posting queue, manage comments, send connection requests, handle DMs, and follow up on replies. Your people don't have to touch it.

How does LinkedIn outbound connect to email?

Both channels run off the same account list and trigger logic. LinkedIn builds the visibility so email doesn't land cold. They work together, not separately.

Can you work with our SDRs and AEs?

Yes. SDRs and AEs get account context, reply summaries, warm handoff notes, and content they can reference. Their outreach gets better because they have something real to point to.

How fast do we get started?

Most teams are live within one to two weeks. We need a clear internal owner, your current ICP, some approved proof points, and access to the accounts we'll be posting from.

What does your team need from us?

A point of contact, insight into your buyers, a list of target accounts, and a review cadence so nothing goes out without your sign-off.

We already work with a content agency. Is this redundant?

Usually not. Most agencies stop at posting. We connect content to outbound, manage the replies, and track pipeline movement. If your current setup is producing meetings, great. If it isn't, this is different.

What does reporting look like?

You see posts shipped, accounts touched, connection accepts, positive replies, meetings booked, and what messages are working best. Everything is tied to sales movement, not vanity metrics.
